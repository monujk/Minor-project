Lotus temple

Overview of Lotus Temple in Delhi The Lotus Temple is a Baha'i-related building that may be seen in New Delhi, the country's capital. One of the most popular destinations in the world, this building's majestic construction expands into the shape of a stunning white petal lotus. Fariborz Sahba, a Canadian architect, created the concept for this shrine, which was finished in 1986. This temple welcomes everyone, regardless of their nationality, religion, ethnicity, or gender, and aims to spread the idea of the Almighty's unity. One of the seven Baha'i Houses of Worship that are spread out around the globe is the Lotus Temple.

Taj mahal

Taj Mahal, Agra: "A Massive Tribute to Love" Overview
One of the seven wonders of the world, Taj Mahal is located on the banks of River Yamuna in Agra. The Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan erected it as a monument to honour Mumtaz Mahal, his third wife. The grave of Shah Jahan himself is also located there. Taj Mahal, one of the most stunning structures in the world, was built in the 17th century using only white marble. Throughout the course of a year, more people than Agra's whole population pass through the beautiful gates to get a sight of this breathtaking structure!

Golden temple
The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is one of India's most spiritual locations and the most important Sikhism site. The spectacular golden architecture of the temple, which is located in the centre of Amritsar, as well as the daily Langar (community kitchen), draw a lot of tourists and worshippers every day. Around 100,000 individuals from all walks of life receive free food at the temple, which is accessible to followers of all religions.

Mysore palace
The Mysore Palace, a majestic structure situated in Mysore in the state of Karnataka, is a truly breathtaking example of Indo-Saracenic style of architecture. It is still the official abode of the royal family of Mysore and is also known as the Amba Vilas Palace. One of the largest palaces in the nation, Mysore Palace was constructed in 1912 for the 24th ruler of the Wodeyar Dynasty.

Fatehpur sikri
The town of Fatehpur Sikri, which is in the Agra district and is 40 kilometres from Agra, is well-known for its tourism attractions. Akbar, the Mughal emperor who created Fatehpur Sikri in 1571, built the majority of the city out of red sandstone. The king basically built it as a walled city, and it served as the imperial capital for fifteen years. The Jodha Bai's Palace, Jama Mosque, Buland Darwaza, and Tomb of Salim Chisti are just a few of the well-known structures found at this location, which is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Imperial Complex is a collection of pavilions that are arranged on a plot of ground in formal geometry and are inspired by tent encampments from the Middle East and Central Asia.

vectoria Memorial
The Victoria Monument is a remnant of the British Raj in India and is situated in the centre of Kolkata, West Bengal. This lavish, white-marbled building, which is practically a copy of the Victoria Monument in London, was erected in Queen Victoria's honour to commemorate her 25 years of rule over India. Hawa Mahal
Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the enormous Hawa Mahal is located at the intersection of the main road in Jaipur, Badi Chaupad. The Hawa Mahal got its name from its unusual design, which is a network of tiny windows that let cool air inside the palace and kept it cool throughout the hot summer months. The palace was built primarily to enable the women of the royal family to participate in the public celebrations while remaining hidden from view in accordance with local custom. It runs all the way to the "zenana" and is situated just on the edge of the Jaipur City Palace.

Shore temple
The Shore Temple, a collection of temples and shrines, was built around 725 AD and faces out onto the Bay of Bengal. It is situated at Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu, India, around 60 kilometres (37 miles) south of Chennai.
